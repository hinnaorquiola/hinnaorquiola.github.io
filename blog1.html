1. Your Website Looks Outdated

Design trends change fast. If your site feels like a relic from five years ago (old fonts, dated layout, or stale color palettes), visitors may perceive your business as less professional. A modern website redesign updates visual language and improves perceived trust — an essential step for attracting international clients and higher-value projects.

Consider a brief brand refresh (typography, color palette, and a simplified layout). See my Website Design & Development services for examples of modern, responsive redesigns.

2. It’s Not Mobile-Friendly

Mobile traffic often accounts for 50%+ of visits. If your site requires pinching, zooming, or has tiny CTAs, you’re losing users and hurting SEO. A mobile-first redesign ensures fast, accessible layouts and clear touch targets so visitors can act quickly.

Tip: test your pages in Chrome DevTools (mobile emulation) and Google’s Mobile-Friendly Test to identify immediate problems.

3. Your Site Loads Slowly

Page speed is a ranking signal and heavily impacts conversions. Slow images, unoptimized scripts, and bloated CSS can all slow down your site. During a redesign I prioritise:

  • Image optimization (WebP + responsive srcset)
  • Lazy loading non-critical assets
  • Minified CSS/JS with critical CSS inlined
  • Lightweight, semantic HTML for better crawlability

Want speed improvements? Check my Website Maintenance and performance optimization offerings.

4. Your Brand Has Outgrown Your Website

If you’ve updated your logo, messaging, or target audience, but the website still tells the “old story,” a redesign realigns visuals and content with your current goals. That helps with conversions and makes your brand more attractive to global clients.

“Your website should evolve as your brand grows — it’s your digital reflection.”

5. You’re Not Getting Inquiries or Sales

If traffic is fine but conversions are low, the issue is often UX or unclear CTAs. A conversion-focused redesign maps the user journey, reduces friction, and adds clear calls to action (contact forms, “book now” buttons, or product CTAs).

Examples of conversion improvements include clearer headline hierarchy, simplified contact forms, and strategic internal linking to high-intent pages like Book or Shop.

6. Navigation Feels Confusing

Visitors should find what they need in 1–3 clicks. If your menu is cluttered, or sections are buried, you’ll lose users. In redesigns I focus on clear IA (information architecture) and mobile navigation patterns that keep important pages discoverable.

7. You’re Embarrassed to Share Your Website

If you hesitate to share your URL, that’s a sign your site isn’t representing you well. A confident, polished site supports outreach, partnerships, and client trust. If you want, I can audit your site and suggest a focused redesign roadmap — get in touch.

8. It Doesn’t Reflect Your Goals Anymore

Whether you’ve scaled services, started selling products, or shifted to coaching, your site should reflect that. A redesign helps prioritize the right content (portfolio, shop, booking) and supports your updated business goals.

FAQ — Quick Answers
